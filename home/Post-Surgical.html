    ❓ Common Questions

    Post-Surgical Care FAQs

    Answers to the most common questions about SwiftNurse Homecare post-surgical support.

    How quickly can SwiftNurse arrange post-surgical care? +
    SwiftNurse Homecare can arrange post-surgical care on the same day as hospital discharge in most cases. We recommend contacting us at least 48 hours before planned discharge to ensure the smoothest transition possible.
    Are SwiftNurse post-surgical carers medically qualified? +
    Yes. All SwiftNurse post-surgical care is delivered by registered nurses (RN) or trained healthcare assistants supervised by registered nurses. Every carer has specialist post-operative care training and full DBS clearance.
    Can SwiftNurse work alongside my NHS care team? +
    Absolutely. SwiftNurse Homecare works seamlessly alongside NHS district nurses, GPs and physiotherapists. We provide detailed care notes and reports to your medical team and can attend hospital follow-up appointments with you.
    How long will I need post-surgical home care? +
    Recovery timelines vary by surgery type and individual patient health. Minor procedures may require 1–2 weeks of support, while major surgeries such as cardiac or orthopaedic operations may benefit from 4–12 weeks of SwiftNurse support. We reassess regularly.
    Is post-surgical home care covered by insurance or NHS funding? +
    Some private health insurance policies cover post-surgical home care. NHS continuing healthcare funding may also be available in some cases. The SwiftNurse Homecare team can help you explore all funding options during your free assessment.
    What happens if my condition changes during recovery? +
    SwiftNurse carers are trained to identify and respond to changes in patient condition. If a concern arises, our clinical team will escalate immediately — contacting your GP, surgical team, or emergency services as appropriate. Your safety is always our priority.
